## Getting Started

### First Launch

When you first open the app, you'll see an interactive map of Canada with various markers representing:
- **Blue markers**: Rivers
- **Cyan markers**: Lakes
- **Green markers**: Bays and sounds
- **Red markers**: Forts
- **Orange markers**: Trading posts
- **Brown markers**: Portages

### Navigation Basics

- **Tap** any marker to learn more about that waterway or location
- **Pinch** to zoom in and out
- **Drag** to pan around the map
- **Tap a waterway** to see its boundary highlighted and automatically zoom to fit

## Exploring the Map

### Waterways (48 total)

The app includes major waterways across Canada:
- **21 Rivers**: St. Lawrence, Mackenzie, Fraser, Ottawa, Saskatchewan, Churchill, Nelson, Hayes, and more
- **12 Lakes**: Superior, Huron, Ontario, Erie, Great Slave, Athabasca, and more
- **11 Bays & Sounds**: Hudson Bay, James Bay, Nootka Sound, and more
- **4 Straits**: Strait of Belle Isle, Juan de Fuca Strait, and more

Each waterway includes:
- Indigenous name and language
- Historical significance
- Associated explorers (displayed chronologically)
- Fur trade information
- Archaeological discoveries

### Historic Locations (84 total)

Explore forts, trading posts, portages, and settlements including:
- **Hudson's Bay Company posts** (York Factory, Fort Edmonton, Norway House)
- **North West Company posts** (Fort William, Grand Portage, Rocky Mountain House)
- **Maritime landing sites** (Cape Bonavista, Cartier's Cross at Gaspé)

### Indigenous Names

Throughout the app, you'll see waterways and locations displayed with both their English and Indigenous names. For example:
- Saskatchewan River (Kisiskatchewan in Cree)
- Athabasca River (derived from Cree)
- Winnipeg (meaning "muddy waters" in Cree)

## Educational Features

### Quizzes

Test your knowledge with educational quizzes:
- **6 main categories**: Explorers, Fur Trade, Maritime History, Indigenous Heritage, Geography, Archaeological Discoveries
- **Grade-specific content**: K-3, 4-6, 7-9, 10-12, and All
- **Difficulty levels**: Easy, Medium, Hard
- **Instant feedback**: See correct answers and explanations after completing

**How to take a quiz:**
1. Navigate to the Quizzes section
2. Filter by category, grade level, or difficulty
3. Select a quiz to begin
4. Answer all questions
5. Submit to see your score and review answers

### Lesson Plans (26 available)

Comprehensive teaching resources for educators and students:
- **Grade-specific**: Lessons for K-3, 4-6, 7-9, and 10-12
- **Curriculum-aligned**: Connected to provincial curriculum expectations
- **Complete plans include**: Learning objectives, activities, discussion questions, assessments, and extension activities

**Featured lesson plans:**
- "The Voyageur Life" (Grade 4-6)
- "Louis Riel and the Métis Nation" (Grade 7-9)
- "Women of the Fur Trade" (Grade 7-9)
- "Mapping Canada: The Work of David Thompson" (Grade 10-12)
- "The Hudson's Bay Company" (Grade 4-6)

### Explorers (38 historical figures)

Learn about explorers spanning 1,000 years of Canadian exploration:
- **Earliest**: Leif Erikson (c. 1000 AD)
- **Most recent**: Joseph Burr Tyrrell (1893)
- **Maritime explorers**: Captain James Cook, Jacques Cartier, Captain George Vancouver
- **Inland explorers**: David Thompson, Alexander Mackenzie, Samuel de Champlain
- **Arctic explorers**: Sir John Franklin, Dr. John Rae, Samuel Hearne

Each profile includes biography, notable achievements, years active, and waterways explored.

### Archaeological Discoveries (8 major finds)

Discover famous archaeological finds:
- **HMS Erebus & Terror** (Franklin expedition ships)
- **Champlain's Astrolabe** (1867 discovery)
- **L'Anse aux Meadows** (Norse settlement, c. 1000 AD)
- **Knight Expedition Remains** (Marble Island)
- **Red Bay Basque Whaling Station**

### Notable Figures (14 profiles)

Highlighting women and Indigenous leaders who shaped Canadian history:
- **Women**: Charlotte Small, Thanadelthur, Marie-Anne Gaboury, Isabel Gunn
- **Indigenous Leaders**: Matonabbee, Saukamappee, Akaitcho, Tattannoeuck, Keskarrah
- **Métis Leaders**: Louis Riel, Gabriel Dumont, Cuthbert Grant, Jean-Baptiste Lagimodière

### Pronunciation Guide (33+ entries)

Learn how to correctly pronounce Indigenous place names, nations, and terms:
- **Place names**: Kisiskatchewan, Athabasca, Manitoba, Assiniboine
- **Nations**: Haudenosaunee, Anishinaabe, Stó:lō
- **Fur trade terms**: Voyageur, Pemmican, Coureur des bois

Each entry includes phonetic spelling and pronunciation tips.

## Interactive Adventures

### Voyageur Journey Simulator

Experience life as a fur trade voyageur through interactive "choose your own adventure" stories:

**Available Journeys:**
1. **"The Grand Portage Route"** (Grade 4-6)
   - Follow the historic fur trade route from Montreal to Grand Portage
   - Make decisions about routes, weather, and trading
   - Learn voyageur songs like "En Roulant Ma Boule"

2. **"Race to the Pacific"** (Grade 7-9)
   - Journey westward during the height of the fur trade
   - Navigate rapids, portages, and encounters with Indigenous guides
   - Experience the challenges of 18th-century exploration

**Features:**
- Historical decision points
- Map integration showing your journey
- Voyageur songs with lyrics
- Educational facts after each segment
- Multiple endings based on your choices
- Progress tracking and scoring

**How to play:**
1. Select a journey from the Voyageur Journeys section
2. Read the introduction and starting scenario
3. Make choices at decision points
4. See the consequences of your decisions
5. Complete the journey to earn points and achievements

### What Happened Here? (GPS Location Feature)

Discover historical events near your current location:

**How it works:**
1. Navigate to "What Happened Here?" section
2. Allow location access when prompted
3. Select search radius (5km, 10km, 25km, 50km, 100km)
4. Filter by category (exploration, fur trade, Indigenous, settlement, battle, treaty)
5. See all historical events within your selected area
6. Tap any event to learn more

**Perfect for:**
- Field trips to historical sites
- Exploring your local area
- Discovering hidden history in your community
- Making connections between past and present

### My Maps - Drawing & Annotation Tools

Create your own custom maps:

**Features:**
- **Pin Tool**: Add custom markers with titles and descriptions
- **Route Drawing**: Draw routes tracing explorer paths or trade routes
- **Notes**: Add text annotations anywhere on the map
- **8 Colors**: Choose from 8 colors for your annotations
- **Save & Share**: Save your maps and generate share codes for classmates
- **Export**: Export map data for reports and presentations

**How to create a map:**
1. Navigate to "My Maps" section
2. Create a new map and give it a title
3. Use the tools to add pins, routes, and notes
4. Customize colors and labels
5. Save your map
6. Share with classmates using a share code

**Ideas for student projects:**
- Trace an explorer's journey
- Map fur trade routes
- Create a treasure hunt
- Mark Indigenous territories
- Compare different explorers' paths

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**Q: Is this app free to use?**
A: Yes, the Canadian Interactive Waterways Initiative is free for all students, teachers, and educational institutions.

**Q: What grade levels is this designed for?**
A: The app contains content for K-12 students, with specific features and activities tailored to different grade levels (K-3, 4-6, 7-9, 10-12).

**Q: Can I use this app offline?**
A: Currently, the app requires an internet connection to access content and maps. Printable resources can be downloaded for offline use.

**Q: Is the app available in French?**
A: Yes! The app supports both English and French. Switch languages in Settings.

### Educational Content

**Q: How accurate is the historical information?**
A: All content is based on historical records and archaeological findings. We work with historians and Indigenous communities to ensure accuracy and cultural sensitivity.

**Q: How are Indigenous names and terms verified?**
A: We consult with Indigenous language experts and communities to ensure proper spellings, pronunciations, and cultural context.

**Q: Can I use this app to meet curriculum requirements?**
A: Yes, lesson plans are aligned with provincial curriculum expectations for Social Studies and Canadian History.

### Gamification

**Q: Do I need to create an account to earn points?**
A: No, progress is tracked using your device ID. Your progress will be saved on the device you're using.

**Q: How do I earn more points?**
A: Complete quizzes, explore waterways, take daily challenges, complete voyageur journeys, and engage with various app features.

**Q: What happens when I reach Master Explorer rank?**
A: You can continue earning points and achievements. We celebrate lifelong learning!

### Technical

**Q: What devices are supported?**
A: The app runs on iOS and Android devices, as well as in web browsers.

**Q: Why isn't my location working for "What Happened Here?"**
A: Make sure you've granted location permissions to the app in your device settings.

**Q: How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?**
A: Use the Feedback section in the app menu or contact your administrator.

### Privacy & Safety

**Q: What information is collected?**
A: We collect minimal data: device ID for progress tracking, optional name for contributions, and location only when you use "What Happened Here?" (and only when granted permission).

**Q: Is my student data safe?**
A: Yes, we follow educational data privacy standards. Student names and progress are stored securely and not shared with third parties.

**Q: Can students communicate with each other through the app?**
A: No, there are no messaging or social features. Students can only share maps using codes.
