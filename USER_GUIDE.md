## Getting Started

When you open the app, you'll see two main tabs at the bottom:

- **Map** - Interactive map of Canada's waterways and historic locations
- **Learn** - Educational resources, lesson plans, and teacher tools

Simply tap on either tab to begin exploring.

---

## Interactive Map

The heart of the app is an interactive map showcasing Canada's major waterways and historic locations.

### Map Features

**Waterway Markers**
- **Blue markers** - Rivers (St. Lawrence, Mackenzie, Fraser, etc.)
- **Cyan markers** - Lakes (Superior, Huron, Winnipeg, etc.)
- **Green markers** - Bays and Sounds (Hudson Bay, Nootka Sound, etc.)

**Historic Location Markers**
- **Red markers** - Forts (Prince of Wales Fort, Fort William, etc.)
- **Orange markers** - Trading Posts (York Factory, Moose Factory, etc.)
- **Brown markers** - Portages and other sites

### How to Use the Map

**Tap any marker** to see a preview card with:
- Name (including Indigenous name if available)
- Type of waterway or location
- Brief description

**Tap "Learn More"** to open the full detail view with:
- Complete history and description
- Connected explorers (displayed chronologically)
- Indigenous heritage information
- Fur trade significance
- Archaeological discoveries
- User-contributed content

### Boundary Highlighting

When you select a waterway:
- The entire waterway boundary illuminates like a highlighter on a map
- The map automatically zooms to show the full extent of the waterway
- This helps students visualize the true scale of Canada's water systems

### Example: Exploring the St. Lawrence River

1. Find the blue marker for "St. Lawrence River" on the map
2. Tap the marker to see the preview
3. Tap "Learn More" to discover:
   - Jacques Cartier's 1534 voyage
   - The Haudenosaunee and Anishinaabe peoples who lived along its shores
   - Samuel de Champlain's exploration
   - The river's role in the fur trade
   - Historic forts and trading posts along the route

## Learn Tab

The Learn tab is your gateway to structured educational content.

### Available Resources

#### Lesson Plans
Browse curriculum-aligned lesson plans organized by:
- **Grade Level**: K-3, 4-6, 7-9, 10-12
- **Subject**: Geography, History, Indigenous Studies, Science, Art
- **Duration**: 30 minutes to full-day activities

Each lesson plan includes:
- Learning objectives
- Required materials
- Step-by-step activities
- Assessment suggestions
- Extension activities

**Example Lesson: "Mapping the Fur Trade Routes" (Grade 4-6)**
- Objective: Students trace the major fur trade routes across Canada
- Activity: Using the app's map, students identify 5 trading posts and the waterways connecting them
- Assessment: Students create their own illustrated map showing one complete trade route

#### Virtual Field Trips
Experience historic locations without leaving the classroom:
- **York Factory** - Walk through the HBC's main depot
- **Fort William** - Attend a North West Company rendezvous
- **L'Anse aux Meadows** - Explore the Norse settlement
- **Prince of Wales Fort** - Tour the massive stone fortress

Each field trip includes:
- 360-degree location views
- Historical context and stories
- Interactive checkpoints
- Reflection questions

#### Document Library
Access primary source documents:
- Historical maps and charts
- Explorer journals and letters
- Trading post records
- Treaty documents
- Photographs and illustrations

**Example Document: "David Thompson's Field Notes"**
- Original journal entries from 1807
- Annotations explaining historical context
- Discussion questions for classroom use

#### Printable Worksheets
Download and print educational materials:
- Map labeling exercises
- Timeline activities
- Vocabulary builders
- Research templates
- Quiz sheets

#### Pronunciation Guide
Learn to correctly pronounce:
- Indigenous place names (e.g., "Haudenosaunee," "Anishinaabe")
- French explorer names (e.g., "La Vérendrye," "Radisson")
- Geographic terms

Each entry includes:
- Audio pronunciation
- Phonetic spelling
- Origin and meaning

#### Historical Comparisons
Compare different aspects of Canadian history:
- Hudson's Bay Company vs. North West Company
- French vs. British exploration approaches
- Different Indigenous nations' relationships with waterways
- Explorer routes across different eras

#### Interactive Timeline
Visualize 1,000 years of exploration history:
- Scroll through events from Leif Erikson (c. 1000 AD) to Joseph Tyrrell (1893)
- Tap any event for detailed information
- Filter by category: Explorers, Forts, Treaties, Discoveries
- See how events connect to specific waterways

## Student Features

### Student Journal
Students can record their learning journey:

**Creating a Journal Entry**
1. Open the Learn tab
2. Tap "My Journal"
3. Tap "New Entry"
4. Select a topic (waterway, explorer, location)
5. Write observations, questions, or reflections
6. Add drawings or photos (optional)
7. Save entry

**Example Journal Entry:**
> **Topic: Hudson Bay**
> **Date: February 11, 2026**
>
> Today I learned that Hudson Bay is named after Henry Hudson who explored it in 1610. I was surprised to learn how many trading posts were built around the bay. The Indigenous peoples had been using these waterways for thousands of years before the Europeans arrived. I want to learn more about the Cree people who lived here.

### Favorite Locations
- Tap the heart icon on any waterway or location to save it
- Access your favorites from the Learn tab
- Great for building research collections

### Achievement Badges
Earn badges for:
- Completing quizzes
- Finishing virtual field trips
- Writing journal entries
- Exploring all waterways in a region
- Learning pronunciation guides

## RCGS Educational Quizzes

Test your knowledge with quizzes developed in partnership with the Royal Canadian Geographical Society.

### Quiz Categories
- **Explorers** - Test knowledge of Canada's famous explorers
- **Fur Trade** - Learn about the trading companies and routes
- **Maritime History** - Discover coastal exploration
- **Indigenous Heritage** - Understand First Nations history
- **Geography** - Know Canada's waterways
- **Archaeological Discoveries** - Learn about famous finds

### Grade Levels
- **K-3**: Picture-based, simplified questions
- **4-6**: Standard multiple choice
- **7-9**: More detailed, analytical questions
- **10-12**: Advanced, source-based questions

### Difficulty Levels
- **Easy**: Basic facts and recognition
- **Medium**: Understanding and application
- **Hard**: Analysis and evaluation

### Taking a Quiz

1. Navigate to Quizzes from the Learn tab
2. Select a category
3. Choose your grade level
4. Pick difficulty
5. Answer multiple choice questions
6. Receive instant feedback with:
   - Your score
   - Correct answers
   - Detailed explanations
   - Links to learn more

**Example Quiz Question (Grade 4-6, Medium):**

> **Question:** Which explorer was the first European to cross North America north of Mexico?
>
> A) Samuel de Champlain
> B) Alexander Mackenzie ✓
> C) David Thompson
> D) Simon Fraser
>
> **Explanation:** Alexander Mackenzie completed the first recorded transcontinental crossing of North America in 1793, reaching the Pacific Ocean at Bella Coola. He painted "from Canada, by land" on a rock at Dean Channel.

### Sample Quizzes Included

The app comes with 6 pre-loaded quizzes containing 36 questions:

4. **Indigenous Waterways** (Grades 4-6)

## Tips for Educators

### Classroom Activities

**1. Explorer Route Challenge**
- Divide class into teams
- Each team traces a different explorer's route on the map
- Teams present their findings to the class
- Discuss how waterways determined exploration routes

**2. Trading Post Simulation**
- Use the map to identify trading posts
- Assign students roles (voyageurs, factors, Indigenous traders)
- Simulate a trading session using historical information from the app

**3. Indigenous Place Names Project**
- Use the pronunciation guide
- Students research the meaning behind Indigenous names
- Create a classroom map with dual names

**4. Timeline Living History**
- Each student is assigned an explorer from the timeline
- Students research using the app
- Class creates a "living timeline" presentation

**5. Archaeological Mystery**
- Present one of the archaeological discoveries
- Students use the app to research the background
- Write a "newspaper article" about the discovery

### Curriculum Connections

The app supports learning outcomes in:
- **Social Studies**: Canadian history, geography, Indigenous studies
- **Science**: Navigation, cartography, environmental science
- **Language Arts**: Research skills, journal writing, oral presentations
- **Art**: Map making, historical illustration
